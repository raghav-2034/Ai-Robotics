# Chapter Design Specification: What is Technology?

## 1. Metadata Profile
*   **Chapter Title**: What is Technology?
*   **Target Class**: Class 1
*   **Target Age**: 5–6 Years
*   **Reading Level**: Early Primary (Max sentence length: 8–10 words, max paragraph: 2–3 lines)
*   **Estimated Learning Time**: 60 Minutes

---

## 2. Learning Goals (Bloom's Taxonomy / "I can..." Statements)
*   **Goal 1**: I can find machines in my home.
*   **Goal 2**: I can explain how machines make work easier.
*   **Goal 3**: I can point out technology inside my classroom.
*   **Goal 4**: I can tell the difference between old tools and new technology.

---

## 3. Story Theme & Character Profiles
*   **Theme**: Leo and his mom discover how tools and smart machines make chores fun.
*   **Characters**:
    *   **Leo**: 5-year-old child. Curious, loves blocks, dislikes cleaning his room.
    *   **Mom**: Encouraging parent. Uses metaphors to explain how things work.
    *   **Miko the Robot**: Leo's smart toy robot companion. Friendly, moves on wheels.

---

## 4. Chapter Flow & Section Breakdown

*   **Cover**: Graphic of Leo, Mom, and Miko.
*   **Learning Goals**: Four colorful checklist items.
*   **Warm-Up**: Interactive prompt: "Look around you. What is moving?"
*   **Story Time**: "Leo's Big Clean-up". Leo uses a broom vs. Mom uses a vacuum cleaner.
*   **Section 1: Machines Around Us**
    *   *Topic*: What is a machine? (A helper tool).
    *   *Examples*: Fridge, toaster, fan, car, clock.
*   **Did You Know?**: Fun fact about how clocks are machines that count time.
*   **Section 2: How Machines Help Us**
    *   *Topic*: Machines save energy and time.
    *   *Examples*: Washing machine vs. washing by hand.
*   **Classroom Activity**: "Soap and Water". Roleplay washing clothes by hand vs using a washing machine.
*   **Section 3: Technology at School**
    *   *Topic*: Tools that help us learn.
    *   *Examples*: Projector, tablet, smartboard, pencil sharpener.
*   **Think Time**: "What would happen if we had no computers in school?"
*   **Section 4: Old vs New Technology**
    *   *Topic*: How machines evolved over time to become smarter.
    *   *Examples*: Letter vs Email, Quill vs Pencil, Fan vs Air Conditioner.
*   **Mini Project**: "My Helper Machine". Building a helper model using paper boxes.
*   **Vocabulary**: Glossary terms (Machine, Technology, Tool, Power, Smart).
*   **Let's Remember**: High-impact summaries.
*   **Quiz**: Extreme-simple MCQs and true/false.
*   **Homework**: Matching lines and coloring tasks.
*   **Teacher Notes**: Timing, pedagogical guidance, misconceptions.

---

## 5. Assessment Plan Summary
*   **Formative**: Section checks (Think Time prompts, coloring pages).
*   **Summative**: Dynamic 2-question quiz (identifying helpers, old vs. new) and paper matching lines.
